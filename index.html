Aisle Six: Where Food Goes to Die.

Supermarkets discard perfectly edible food because of cosmetic standards and "sell-by" dates that carry no legal meaning. In the EU alone, retail accounts for 5% of food waste — around nine million tons a year.

A banana with one brown spot. A yogurt two days from its date. A dented can. All binned, despite being safe and nutritious. The rules are written for shelves, not stomachs.

"We do not have a food shortage. We have a food distribution catastrophe."

That waste carries a carbon footprint equivalent to 37 million cars on the road. If food waste were a country, it would be the third-largest emitter of greenhouse gases on the planet, after China and the United States.

Your Fridge Is a Graveyard.

A

Households are the single largest source of food waste in high-income countries — bigger than restaurants, supermarkets, and farms combined.

Interactive fridge illustration with three drawers; each drawer, when opened, reveals labels of forgotten food like spinach 14 days old, milk 8 days old, raspberries 9 days old.
B

The average European household wastes 70 kg of food per person per year. Roughly €700 straight into the bin.

C

Most of it isn't leftovers. It's food we bought and never opened.
