## Problem 01 — The Supermarket

Eyebrow: `Problem 01 — The Supermarket`  
Title: **Aisle Six: Where Food Goes to Die.** (amber)

1. Supermarkets discard **perfectly edible food** because of cosmetic standards and sell-by dates that have no legal basis. In the EU alone, retail accounts for **5% of food waste** — nine million tons a year.
2. A banana with one brown spot. A yogurt two days from its date. A dented can. **All binned**, despite being safe and nutritious.
3. (Pull quote — gold, Fraunces italic)  
> *"We do not have a food shortage. We have a food distribution catastrophe."*
4. That waste carries a carbon footprint equivalent to **37 million cars** on the road. Food waste, if it were a country, would be the **third-largest emitter** of greenhouse gases on the planet — after China and the US.

## Problem 02 — The Home Fridge

Eyebrow: `Problem 02 — The Home Fridge`  
Title: **Your Fridge Is a Graveyard.** (amber)

- Panel A: **Households** are the single largest source of food waste in high-income countries — bigger than restaurants, supermarkets and farms combined.
- Panel B: The average European household wastes **70 kg of food per person**, per year. Roughly **€700 in the bin.**
- Panel C: Most of it isn't leftovers. It's food we bought and **never opened.**

## Solutions

Eyebrow: `Solutions — Three shifts` (moss)  
Title: **Not Every Problem Needs a Miracle.** (moss)

Intro: This one needs **habits.** Here are three shifts, ordered from easiest to biggest impact. None of them require you to become a different person.

**Card 1 · EFFORT: LOW**  
Front: *Shop with a list.*  
Back number: **–20%**  
Back: Households that shop with a list waste up to 20% less food. Plan meals for 3–4 days, not 7. *Buy less, more often.*

**Card 2 · EFFORT: MEDIUM**  
Front: *Love your leftovers.*  
Back number: **#1**  
Back: The easiest climate action in your kitchen: eat what you already bought. *"Leftover Friday"* is a rule worth stealing.

**Card 3 · EFFORT: ONE TAP**  
Front: *Apps that rescue food.*  
Back number: **90M**  
Back: Rescue apps connect you to bakeries, restaurants and neighbors giving away surplus food at near-zero cost. *90 million users and counting.*
