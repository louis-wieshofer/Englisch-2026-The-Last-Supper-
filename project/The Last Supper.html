Problem 01    The Supermarket

Aisle Six:
Where Food Goes
to Die.

ITEMS WASTED IN THIS SESSION 0

Supermarkets discard perfectly edible food because of cosmetic standards and sell-by dates that have no legal basis. In the EU alone, retail accounts for 5% of food waste — nine million tons a year.

A banana with one brown spot. A yogurt two days from its date. A dented can. All binned, despite being safe and nutritious.

“We do not have a food shortage. We have a food distribution catastrophe.”

That waste carries a carbon footprint equivalent to 37 million cars on the road. Food waste, if it were a country, would be the third-largest emitter of greenhouse gases on the planet — after China and the US.
